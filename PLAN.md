# Polish checklists, fix score boost copy, and redesign Benefits like Guides

## Checklists — clean wrapping, no awkward splits or "…"

- Throughout every checklist (Benefits action items, Readiness tasks, Roadmap, Documents, etc.), task text will wrap to up to 2 lines by default — never with a "…" cut-off.
- Tapping any checklist row expands it to show the full text and the "How to do this" steps underneath.
- Row heights grow naturally to fit the text, so words never get split mid-card.

## Readiness — remove the "+X% to score" phrase and make actions live

- Remove all "+X% to category" / "boost your score by X%" copy from the Readiness screen.
- The "Next Best Actions" list will now jump the user straight to the exact place to take that action (the specific benefit step, the specific document, or the specific checklist item) instead of just toggling a checkbox.
- Subtext under each action becomes a short, plain-English nudge (e.g. "Upload your DD-214" or "Start your VA disability claim") rather than a percentage promise.

## Benefits — redesigned to feel positive and educational, matching Guides

- The Benefits list now uses the same full-width hero card style as Guides: large colored icon tile, title, 1-line teaser, and a chevron.
- The big "0/X" counters on each card are removed so nothing feels negative when you're just getting started. Progress only appears subtly once you've actually completed steps.
- Each Benefit detail page is restructured to read like a guide with clear sections:
  - **What's available** — plain-English summary of the benefit
  - **Who qualifies** — eligibility in everyday terms
  - **How to get it** — numbered steps you can act on
  - **Where to apply / official sources** — direct links to the official agency
  - **Action checklist** — kept at the bottom, but now wraps cleanly and links to the right tool when relevant
- Tone shifts from "checklist of things you haven't done" to "here's what's available and how to claim it."

## Consistency pass

- Same wrap-to-2-lines + expand-on-tap rule applied to the Roadmap checklist, Documents checklist, and First Year/First 30 Days checklists so the experience feels uniform.
